## Problem Section

Headline:

Every platform wants a different marketer.

Copy:

Local owners are expected to understand TikTok trends, Instagram aesthetics, Facebook community behavior, and Xiaohongshu search culture. Most do not have the time, budget, or team to do that every week.

Pain points:

- Posting is inconsistent.
- Cross-posted content feels generic.
- Trends are hard to judge.
- Xiaohongshu requires native cultural strategy.
- Existing tools track likes, but owners care about bookings, visits, calls, and sales.

## Solution Section

Headline:

Upload once. Get a weekly growth plan.

Copy:

LocalPilot AI learns your business, your location, your customers, and your goals. Then it creates platform-specific campaigns, explains why they should work, and tracks what brings real customer demand.

Steps:

1. Add your business profile.
2. Upload a video, product, service, menu item, or promotion.
3. Review platform-native campaigns.
4. Approve direct or assisted publishing.
5. Improve next week based on real results.

## Feature Section

### Platform-Native Campaigns

Same business input, different platform strategy.

TikTok gets the hook. Instagram gets the visual story. Facebook gets the community post. Xiaohongshu gets the searchable Chinese-language note.

### Xiaohongshu Growth Engine

Create RED-native content with Chinese copy, 种草 framing, keyword search structure, hashtags, cover text, save-oriented formatting, KOL/KOC brief support, and compliance checks.

### Trend-To-Action Analyzer

See which trends are useful, risky, irrelevant, or high opportunity for your specific business.

### Local Competitor Watcher

Track nearby businesses, content themes, offers, posting rhythm, and engagement signals.

### ROI Loop For Local Businesses

Track calls, bookings, DMs, QR scans, coupon redemptions, saves, Google Maps clicks, and website visits.

### Assisted Publishing

Use direct publishing where platform APIs allow it. For restricted platforms, receive a ready-to-post package with video, caption, hashtags, cover text, and checklist.

## Visual Direction Options

### Option 1: Operator Command Center

Best for VC and SaaS credibility.

Look:

- Dark charcoal or deep ink background.
- Crisp white typography.
- Glassy but restrained dashboard panels.
- Accent colors inspired by TikTok cyan/red, Instagram magenta, Facebook blue, Xiaohongshu red.
- Hero product preview feels like a real AI operating console.

Strength:

Makes the product feel powerful, technical, and investor-ready.

### Option 2: Local Growth Studio

Best for small business customers.

Look:

- Bright off-white background.
- Warm photography-style product moments.
- Friendly dashboard cards.
- Green, coral, and blue accents.
- More human, less enterprise.

Strength:

Feels approachable to restaurant owners, salons, clinics, shops, and local service businesses.

### Option 3: Cross-Cultural Growth Engine

Best for emphasizing the Xiaohongshu wedge.

Look:

- Clean white base with bold red, black, cyan, and soft pink accents.
- Bilingual English/Chinese UI details.
- Platform map, local discovery, KOC/KOL campaign examples.
- Strong emphasis on "TikTok + Instagram + Facebook + Xiaohongshu."

Strength:

Clearly separates the product from ContentStudio-style Western social schedulers.

## Recommendation

Start with Option 1 for investor credibility, but include Option 3's Xiaohongshu advantage in the copy and product preview.
